## 1. 市场与客户调研

- 目标市场选择：分析目标国家/地区的市场规模、需求结构、关税与非关税壁垒、文化与消费习惯。
- 竞争与价格分析：同类产品竞争者、价格区间、主流销售渠道（分销、代理、电商、B2B平台）。
- 客户资格核查：企业资质（营业执照、税务登记）、经营年限、主要产品、历史交易记录、信用与付款习惯。
- 合规与准入：目标市场是否有特殊认证（CE、FDA、CCC、RoHS 等），是否涉及配额或禁运。

工具与渠道：海关数据、阿里巴巴/GlobalSources/印度贸易平台、贸易展会、行业报告、LinkedIn 与客户官网。

## 2. 报价与合同

- 报价构成：产品成本（材料、人工）、制造费用、包装、内陆运输、出口清关费、保险、预计关税、利润、汇率影响与报价有效期。
- 报价类型：FOB、CIF、EXW 等（参见第3节贸易术语）。
- 样品与MOQ：样品费用、交货周期、最小起订量（MOQ）。
- 付款方式：电汇（T/T）、信用证（L/C）、托收（D/P、D/A）、第三方支付（PayPal、Escrow）、开立押汇等。
- 合同要点：产品规格、数量、单价、包装要求、交货期、验收标准、付款条款、违约责任、不可抗力、仲裁/管辖法院、保密条款、知识产权保护。

实务建议：报价时写清报价币种与价格含义（例如：USD 10/pcs，FOB 上海），并标注有效期限与交期。

## 3. 贸易术语（Incoterms）简要

- EXW（Ex Works）：卖方在工厂交货，买方承担大部分费用与风险。
- FOB（Free On Board）：卖方承担到装运港并将货物装上船的费用与风险。
- CIF（Cost, Insurance and Freight）：卖方负责运费与保险，风险在装船时转移，但卖方承担到达目的港的运费与最低保险。
- DAP / DDP：卖方负责运至目的地（DDP 包含进口清关与缴税）。

选择原则：根据公司控制能力、物流网络与风险偏好选择合适术语。小买家或不熟悉流程者可选择卖方承担更多责任的条款。

## 4. 运输与报关

- 货运方式：海运（整柜/拼箱）、空运、陆运、快递（DHL/UPS/FedEx）。选择依据：时效、成本、货物特性。
- 订舱（Booking）：与货代确认舱位、ETD/ETA、截关/截单时间、所需单证。
- 提单（Bill of Lading, B/L）：海运重要单据，分为直提提单（直达）和转运、可转让不可转让等。提单是货权凭证。
- 报关流程：出口报关（申报商品编码、重量、数量、出口退税资料）、进口报关（HS code、申报价值、原产地证、关税与增值税）。
- 商检：部分商品需要商检或认证，需提前申请并预留时间。

提示：确保商品申报的 HS Code 与商业发票一致，避免产生海关争议和罚款。

## 5. 单证与合规

常用单证：商业发票（Commercial Invoice）、装箱单（Packing List）、提单（B/L）或空运运单（AWB）、原产地证（CO）、保险单、质量证书、检验证书、合同复印件、相关许可证。

- 发票要点：卖方与买方信息、商品描述（英文/目标市场语言）、数量、单价、总价、贸易术语、原产地、发票号与日期、付款条款。
- 装箱单要点：箱数、箱号、毛重/净重、尺寸、包装方式、每箱品名/数量。
- 原产地证：根据目的国要求申请，直接影响关税待遇。

合规重点：反洗钱/反恐融资、制裁与出口管制（如受管制目的地或最终用户核查）、产品安全与化学品限值。

## 6. 收款与结算

- 常见方式：T/T（电汇）、信用证（L/C）、D/P、D/A、第三方托收、国际保理、Payoneer/PayPal。
- 信用证要点：不可撤销信用证（Irrevocable L/C）、跟单信用证（Documentary L/C），注意装运期限、单证一致性、可分/可转等条款。
- 风险控制：对新客户要求预付款（如30%订金），分阶段付款；对长期客户可考虑信用额度或保理服务。
- 汇率风险：锁定汇率（远期合约）、以外币计价、设置汇率调整条款。

建议：严格按照合同与单据要求操作单证，避免因单证不符导致银行拒付或延迟收款。

## 7. 质量管理与售后

- 质量控制点：来料检验、过程检验、出货检验（AQL标准）、第三方检验（SGS/Intertek等）。
- 质量纠纷处理：明确检验依据与争议解决流程；保留照片、检验报告、测试数据作为证据。
- 售后服务：保修条款、退换货政策、技术支持、客户反馈与改进记录。

实务建议：对关键客户和高价值订单，采用第三方检验并在装运前取得检验报告。

## 8. 风险与合规注意事项

- 贸易制裁与禁运：核查目的地、最终用户与商品是否在制裁名单或有出口限制。
- 反贿赂与合规：建立合规政策，员工与代理要签署合规承诺，避免向政府官员或代理当中存在利益冲突的付款。
- 税务与转移定价：注意长期跨国交易的税务影响与关联公司交易规范。
- 保险风险：推荐购买货运保险（至少按 CIP/CIF 的最低保险标准），并考虑运营性险种（信用保险、政治风险保险）。

## 9. 常用英文短语与模板

- 报价邮件开头："Dear [Name], Thank you for your inquiry. Please find our quotation below for your reference." 
- 报价条目示例："Unit Price: USD 10.00 per piece, MOQ: 500 pcs, Lead Time: 30 days after deposit." 
- 追单/催款："Please be reminded that the payment due date is [date]. We kindly request payment to avoid shipment delays."

## 10. 业务流程清单（快速检查表）

- 核实客户资质与信用
- 确认产品规格、包装与MOQ
- 报价并约定贸易术语与付款方式
- 签订合同并收取订金（如适用）
- 生产监控与质检
- 订舱与准备出口单证

- 装运并提供提单/运单
- 提供单证并催收尾款
- 售后跟进与客户回访

## 1. Market & Customer Research

- Market selection: analyze market size, demand structure, tariff and non-tariff barriers, culture and consumer habits of target countries/regions.
- Competition & pricing analysis: competitors, price ranges, main sales channels (distribution, agents, e-commerce, B2B platforms).
- Customer due diligence: company qualifications (business license, tax registration), years of operation, main products, trade history, credit and payment habits.
- Compliance & market access: whether target market requires special certifications (CE, FDA, CCC, RoHS, etc.), quotas or bans.

Sources & channels: customs data, Alibaba/GlobalSources/India platforms, trade shows, industry reports, LinkedIn and company websites.

## 2. Quotation & Contracts

- Quotation components: product cost (materials, labor), manufacturing overhead, packing, inland transport, export customs fees, insurance, estimated duties, profit margin, currency exposure and quotation validity.
- Quotation types: FOB, CIF, EXW, etc. (see section 3).
- Samples & MOQ: sample fees, lead time, minimum order quantity (MOQ).
- Payment methods: T/T, L/C, documentary collections (D/P, D/A), third-party payments (PayPal, Escrow), factoring, etc.
- Contract key points: product specifications, quantity, unit price, packing requirements, delivery time, acceptance criteria, payment terms, liabilities, force majeure, arbitration/jurisdiction, confidentiality, IP protection.

Practical tip: clearly state currency and price terms (e.g., USD 10/pcs, FOB Shanghai), quotation validity and lead time.

## 3. Trade Terms (Incoterms) — brief

- EXW (Ex Works): seller delivers at factory; buyer bears most costs and risks.
- FOB (Free On Board): seller handles costs and risks until goods are loaded on board at a named port.
- CIF (Cost, Insurance and Freight): seller pays cost, freight and insurance to the destination port; risk transfers when goods are loaded on board.
- DAP / DDP: seller is responsible to deliver to the named place (DDP includes import clearance and duties).

Selection: choose terms based on your logistics capability, network and risk appetite. Small buyers or inexperienced importers often prefer sellers to take more responsibility.

## 4. Transportation & Customs

- Modes: sea (FCL/LCL), air, land, express courier (DHL/UPS/FedEx). Choose based on time, cost and product characteristics.
- Booking: confirm space, ETD/ETA, cut-off times, and required documents with the freight forwarder.
- Bill of Lading (B/L): key sea freight document; negotiable/non-negotiable, direct/via transshipment. B/L is a document of title.
- Customs procedures: export declaration (HS code, weight, quantity, export rebate paperwork), import declaration (HS code, declared value, CO, duties and VAT).
- Inspection: some goods require pre-shipment inspection or certification—allow time to apply.

Tip: ensure HS codes match commercial invoices to avoid customs disputes and fines.

## 5. Documents & Compliance

Common documents: Commercial Invoice, Packing List, Bill of Lading or AWB, Certificate of Origin (CO), Insurance Policy, Quality Certificate, Inspection Certificate, copy of the contract, and relevant licenses.

- Invoice points: seller/buyer details, goods description (in English/target language), quantity, unit price, total amount, Incoterm, origin, invoice number & date, payment terms.
- Packing list points: number of packages, package numbers, gross/net weight, dimensions, packing method, content per package.
- Certificate of Origin: apply as required; impacts duty treatment.

Compliance focus: AML/CFT, export controls & sanctions (check destination and end-user), product safety and chemical limits.

## 6. Payment & Settlement

- Common methods: T/T, L/C, D/P, D/A, documentary collections, international factoring, Payoneer/PayPal.
- L/C highlights: irrevocable vs. revocable, documentary L/C; watch for shipment deadlines, document consistency, divisible/transferable clauses.
- Risk control: require deposits (e.g., 30%) from new customers, staged payments; for long-term clients consider credit limits or factoring.
- Currency risk: hedge with forward contracts, price in stable currency, include currency adjustment clauses.

Advice: follow contract & document requirements strictly to avoid bank rejection or delayed payments.

## 7. Quality Management & After-Sales

- QC points: incoming material inspection, in-process inspection, pre-shipment inspection (AQL standards), third-party inspections (SGS/Intertek).
- Dispute handling: specify inspection standards and dispute resolution; keep photos, inspection reports and test data as evidence.
- After-sales: warranty terms, return/exchange policy, technical support, customer feedback and improvement logs.

Practical: use third-party inspection for key orders and obtain reports before shipment.

## 8. Risks & Compliance Considerations

- Trade sanctions & embargoes: screen destination, end-users and products against sanction lists and export control restrictions.
- Anti-bribery & compliance: implement compliance policies, require agents/employees to sign commitments, avoid payments that may create conflicts of interest with officials.
- Tax & transfer pricing: consider tax implications of long-term cross-border activities and related-party transactions.
- Insurance: recommend cargo insurance (at least minimum per CIP/CIF), consider credit insurance and political risk coverage.

## 10. Business Process Checklist (Quick Checklist)

- Verify customer qualifications & credit
- Confirm product specs, packing & MOQ
- Quote and agree on Incoterms & payment
- Sign contract and collect deposit (if applicable)
- Monitor production & QC
- Book shipment and prepare export documents
- Export customs & inspection (if required)
- Ship and provide B/L or AWB
- Submit documents and collect final payment
- After-sales follow-up and customer visit
